Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

In short: Your data stays on your device. We do not have access to it. Period.

What we collect

Very little. Here is the exhaustive list:

  • @keepid.me email aliases: the mapping between your alias and your forwarding email address is stored on our server (hosted on Turso) to enable email forwarding.

That's it. Nothing else leaves your device.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your real name, first name, or identity
  • Your postal address or phone number
  • Your passwords (neither yours nor the generated ones)
  • Your browsing history
  • The websites where you use KeepID
  • No tracking, analytics, or advertising data
  • No cookies

Where your data lives

On your device, encrypted.

All your fictional identities, passwords, personal data, and settings are stored in your browser's local storage (chrome.storage.local), encrypted with AES-256-GCM.

The encryption key is derived from your master password via PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations, SHA-256). It is never stored in plaintext — it is recomputed on every unlock and kept in session memory only.

Who can access your data

Nobody but you.

  • Our servers only contain the alias → forwarding email mapping. Not your identities, not your passwords.
  • Your browser stores the encrypted vault. Without your master password, the data is unreadable.
  • KeepID (the company) has no technical means to access your vault. This is the Zero-Knowledge principle.

⚠️ Important: If you forget your master password, we cannot recover your data. There is no recovery procedure. This is the price of Zero-Knowledge security.

Breach verification (HIBP)

KeepID uses the Have I Been Pwned API to check whether your emails or passwords have been compromised in data breaches. This check uses the k-anonymity protocol: only the first 5 characters of the SHA-1 hash are sent to the API. Your full email or password is never transmitted.

Your rights (GDPR)

In accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the following rights:

  • Right of access: Settings → Export my data
  • Right of rectification: Edit your data directly in the extension
  • Right of erasure: Settings → Delete my account (full and irreversible deletion)
  • Right of portability: The export generates a standard JSON file

Subprocessors

  • Turso (database): hosts the alias → forwarding email mapping. Headquarters: San Francisco, data on European servers.
  • Scaleway (API hosting, VPS Paris, France): hosts the email routing API. Data in transit only.
  • Mailgun EU (email reception and sending, servers in Frankfurt, Germany): processes the reception and forwarding of incoming emails to your aliases.
  • Mistral AI (form analysis, France): analyzes the structure of web forms to enable automatic filling. No personal data is transmitted — only the form HTML is sent.
