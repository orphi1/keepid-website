1. Our philosophy

KeepID is designed to protect you preventively. Our architecture is zero-knowledge: your vault encryption key is derived locally from your master password and is never stored server-side. Technically, KeepID (the company) has no means to access the contents of your vault.

On every form, you decide what level of information to share: full fictional identity, mixed mode (email alias + real identity), or real data. Control stays in your hands.

3. What data we process

3.1 Always collected

  • Forwarding email address: used solely to forward emails received on your aliases. Stored on our European infrastructure with restricted access.
  • Master password hash: derived via PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations, SHA-256). The plaintext password is never transmitted nor stored.
  • Alias mappings @keepid.me → real email: stored on Turso EU to enable routing of incoming emails.

3.2 Depending on the mode you choose

  • KeepID mode (full fictional identity): no real personal data is transmitted to Mistral. Only the form HTML is analysed.
  • Mixed mode (email alias + real identity): your real first name, last name, phone number and address are transmitted to Mistral to generate a coherent fill.
  • Real mode: all your real information is transmitted to Mistral for filling.

In all cases, the Zero Data Retention (ZDR) mode enabled on our Mistral account ensures no retention beyond the strict processing time.

3.3 Never collected

  • No analytics, no tracking cookies, no pixels.
  • No behavioural data (clicks, navigation, time spent).
  • No content of forwarded emails is read or stored by KeepID: emails transit through our forwarding processor (Mailgun EU, under GDPR-compliant DPA) then are relayed to your real inbox without human intervention.

5. AI form analysis (Mistral)

KeepID uses Mistral AI to analyse the HTML structure of web forms and generate filling appropriate to your selected mode.

The Zero Data Retention mode is enabled on our Mistral account. In practice:

  • No request sent to Mistral is retained beyond the strict processing time.
  • No request is used to train their models.
  • Requests are processed primarily on Mistral's European infrastructure.

In rare cases of technical fallback, processing outside the EU may occur. The ZDR no-storage guarantee applies in all cases.

The content transmitted to Mistral depends on the chosen mode (see section 3.2). password fields are systematically filtered out before sending.

6. Your GDPR rights

In accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access: obtain the list of data we process about you.
  • Right of rectification: correct inaccurate data about you.
  • Right of erasure: request the deletion of your data.
  • Right of portability: retrieve your data in a structured format (JSON).
  • Right to object: object to processing under certain conditions.
  • Right to restriction: request the suspension of a processing activity.

These rights can be exercised directly from the extension (Settings → Export / Delete my data) or by emailing dpo@keepid.fr. Maximum response time: 1 month (Article 12 GDPR).

You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority. In France, this is the CNIL: www.cnil.fr.

7. Security

  • AES-256-GCM encryption of the local vault.
  • Key derived from the master password via PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations) with a unique salt per user.
  • Master password never stored or transmitted (zero-knowledge architecture).
  • HMAC-SHA256 validation of all incoming webhooks.
  • HTTPS everywhere (Let's Encrypt certificates renewed automatically).
  • Strong 2FA enabled on all administrative accounts of backend services.
  • <input type="password"> fields are entirely removed from the HTML before any backend transmission. The backend then only extracts the structure of fields (name, type, label), never their value, before sending to Mistral for analysis.
  • Zero Data Retention mode enabled at Mistral AI.

⚠️ Important: if you forget your master password, we technically cannot recover your data. This is the price of zero-knowledge security — remember to keep your recovery code.
